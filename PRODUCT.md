# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Praxisplaner serves patients booking appointments directly in a hosted SaaS portal and practice teams who manage availability, scheduling rules, staff, locations, appointments, absences, and PVS-linked patient data. Patients use it in a high-trust healthcare context where the next step must be obvious and confidence matters. Practice members use it during operational work, often while coordinating appointments, importing GDT context, resolving scheduling constraints, and adjusting rule sets under time pressure.

## Product Purpose

Praxisplaner makes appointment availability explicit, versioned, and bookable. It lets a medical practice define scheduling rules, simulate changes, activate rule sets, manage appointment resources, and offer direct patient booking without losing the operational truth held in the practice's PVS. Success means patients can complete the correct booking path without confusion, while practice teams can see why a slot is available or blocked and change scheduling behavior without hidden side effects.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, clinical, and trustworthy, with enough product character to avoid a generic Vercel-style SaaS surface. The interface should feel current and intentional: sharper corners, controlled density, clear states, and a poppy accent color used with discipline for primary actions, selection, and meaningful status.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic monochrome SaaS minimalism, especially interfaces that feel like a default Vercel template with no healthcare-specific judgment. Avoid over-rounded cards, pill-heavy layouts, decorative gradients, soft ghost-card shadows, playful medical illustrations, and marketing-page composition inside task surfaces. Avoid sterile complexity that makes patients feel they are operating internal practice software.

## Design Principles

1. Make scheduling logic inspectable: users should understand which rule, resource, or occupancy fact produced a booking outcome.
2. Keep patient booking calm and linear: each screen should make the next safe action unmistakable.
3. Treat practice workflows as expert tools: support density, comparison, undo, simulation, and clear state changes without decorative friction.
4. Use accent color as operational signal: reserve high-chroma color for primary actions, current context, warnings, confirmations, and selected objects.
5. Prefer sharp clarity over softness: corners, spacing, and component states should feel precise rather than rounded, bubbly, or template-like.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA as the baseline. Preserve keyboard access for every workflow, visible focus states, sufficient text and state contrast, readable German healthcare copy, reduced-motion alternatives, and form error messages that identify the field, the problem, and the corrective action.
